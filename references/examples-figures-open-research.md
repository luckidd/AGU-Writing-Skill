# Figure and Open Research Writing Patterns

Use these structure-only patterns for figure captions and Availability Statements. Replace every bracketed field with author-provided and verified details. Never invent a repository, identifier, version, license, or access condition.

## Multi-panel observation caption

`Figure [N]. [Purpose or overview]. (a) [Quantity] in [units] from [source]. (b) [Quantity] as a function of [coordinates]. (c) [Derived diagnostic] calculated using [method]. [Colors/lines/symbols] denote [meaning], and shading marks [interval or regime]. Missing values are shown as [treatment].`

## Model comparison caption

`Figure [N]. Comparison of [observed quantity] and [modeled quantity] for [regime]. [Style] denotes observations, and [style] denotes model configuration [name]. Panels show [dimensions or cases]. Error bars or shading represent [uncertainty], and [metric] is reported for [scope].`

## Data availability

`The [data type] used for [purpose] is available from [repository] at [DOI or persistent URL] under [license and access conditions] ([formal data citation]).`

## Software availability

`Version [version] of [software] used for [purpose] is preserved at [repository] via [DOI or persistent URL] under [license and access conditions] and is developed at [development-platform URL] ([formal software citation]).`

## Processed products

`The processed data and scripts supporting [figures/results] are preserved at [repository] via [DOI or persistent URL] under [license and access conditions].`

For submission-ready text, verify the target object, DOI or URL, version, license, access, and formal citation. A development-platform URL does not replace preservation of a release.
