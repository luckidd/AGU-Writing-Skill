# Main-Text Writing Patterns

Use these structure-only patterns for Introduction, Results, Discussion, and Conclusions. Replace bracketed fields with author-provided or verified evidence. Do not convert association into causation or use a template's mechanism as evidence.

## Introduction

### Context and evidence synthesis

`[System or phenomenon] is governed by [relevant processes] and affects [bounded consequence]. Previous studies establish [known result supported by citations], while [second evidence class] emphasizes [alternative or complementary process]. These results do not yet determine [precise unresolved quantity or distinction] because [coverage, method, or physical limitation].`

### Gap and present study

`The remaining uncertainty concerns [mechanism, magnitude, regime, or predictive capability]. Resolving it requires [diagnostic or comparison] that distinguishes [alternative A] from [alternative B]. Here we [analyze/model/derive/evaluate] [data or system] over [sample or regime] to [test/quantify/separate] [specific relationship or process].`

## Results

### Finding-led paragraph

`[Main result] under [condition or regime] (Figure [X]). [Quantity] changes from [supplied value] to [supplied value] over [interval/domain], whereas [comparison quantity] shows [contrast]. The agreement or sequence between [diagnostics] [indicates/suggests] [immediate bounded interpretation]. This relationship weakens or cannot be evaluated under [boundary].`

### Model-observation or statistical comparison

`The model reproduces [observed feature] but not [failure feature]. Agreement is strongest for [regime] according to [metric], whereas errors increase under [condition]. [Sensitivity or robustness test] supports [specific implemented contribution or relationship] but does not exclude [material alternative].`

## Discussion

### Central interpretation and alternatives

`The combined evidence [supports/indicates/suggests] that [process] contributes to [phenomenon] under [conditions]. This interpretation follows from [diagnostic 1] together with [diagnostic 2], rather than [single ambiguous observation] alone. An alternative explanation is [mechanism or bias], which would predict [distinguishing signature]. The available [diagnostic] [reduces/does not remove] this ambiguity.`

### Prior work, limitation, and implication

`The result agrees with prior evidence for [established relationship] but extends it to [verified regime or diagnostic]. The difference from [other result] is consistent with [supported difference]; without [missing test], it should not be assigned to [stronger mechanism]. This conclusion is limited by [specific limitation], which affects [claim or regime]. Within the tested conditions, the result supports [bounded change in understanding, modeling, measurement, or operations].`

## Conclusions

`We used [evidence or approach] to determine [objective] in [regime]. The analysis yields [two to five] findings: [results with conditions and diagnostics]. Together, they [support/indicate] [central conclusion] for [scope]. [Specific limitation] remains the main constraint on extension to [excluded or future domain].`

Do not introduce a new result, mechanism, or citation-dependent argument in the Conclusions.
