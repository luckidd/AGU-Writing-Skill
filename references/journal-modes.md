# Journal Modes

Apply only differences that affect framing or required elements. Treat official requirements as higher priority than these writing preferences.

| Mode | Writing preference | Journal-specific emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| AGU Advances | Full-length, cross-disciplinary, high-impact argument | Make the conceptual advance and value to more than one Earth and space science community explicit. Plain Language Summary (PLS) is required. |
| Geophysical Research Letters (GRL) | Short, timely, high-signal | State the advance early and remove peripheral analysis. Abstract under 150 words; maximum 12 publication units; PLS required. |
| JGR: Space Physics | Complete specialist evidence chain | Provide sufficient data, methods, diagnostics, validation, competing explanations, and limitations. PLS is optional under current general AGU guidance. |
| Space Weather | Application- and user-facing | State the operational target, baseline, validation regime, performance, failure cases, latency or coverage limits, and user consequence. PLS required. |
| Earth and Space Science | Data, methods, instruments, models, and reusable research products | Emphasize validation, reproducibility, documentation, limitations, and reuse value. |
| Radio Science | Electromagnetic propagation, sensing, antennas, measurement, and modeling | Make theory-measurement correspondence, assumptions, calibration, uncertainty, and technical reproducibility explicit. |
| Generic AGU | General Earth and space science | Apply AGU-wide clarity and compliance only; do not invent journal-specific priorities. |

## Rule classes

- **Required:** current AGU submission requirement.
- **Journal-specific:** requirement or limit that applies to a named journal or article type.
- **Writing preference:** editorial strategy, not a formal requirement.
