# Front-Matter Writing Patterns

Use these structure-only patterns for titles, Abstracts, Key Points, and Plain Language Summaries. Replace every bracketed field with author-provided or verified content. Never reuse implied facts, mechanisms, numbers, or citations.

## Titles

- Result-led: `[Physical process] controls [measured response] under [bounded regime]`
- Relationship: `[Quantity A] dependence of [quantity B] in [system or region]`
- Mechanism test: `Testing [candidate mechanism] for [phenomenon] using [evidence type]`
- Statistical: `[Statistical characteristic] of [quantity] under [verified conditions]`
- Model or data product: `Quantifying [process] with [validated model-data approach]`
- Forecast: `Forecasting [target] at [lead time] under [validation scope]`

Use causal verbs only when the evidence supports causality. Avoid unsupported priority words such as `first`, `novel`, and `unprecedented`.

## Abstracts

### Observational or event study

`[Phenomenon] affects [system or consequence], but [specific unresolved question]. We use [observations and derived quantities] during [event/sample/regime] to determine [question]. [Principal result with supplied magnitude and condition]. [Independent diagnostic] shows [second result]. Together, the results [indicate/suggest] [bounded interpretation], while [limitation or alternative] restricts extension to [excluded regime].`

### Statistical study

`The [distribution/variability/relationship] of [quantity] is important for [reason], yet [quantification or coverage gap]. We analyze [data source] over [verified interval/sample] using [selection and statistical method]. [Quantity] varies with [condition] by [supplied result], and [second relationship] remains [status] under [robustness test]. The results [support/indicate] [bounded inference] for [domain], subject to [sampling or methodological boundary].`

### Model, theory, or space-weather application

`[Model, theory, or operational problem] remains uncertain because [specific limitation]. We [develop/apply/evaluate] [approach] using [constraints, baseline, or validation design]. [Primary quantitative result under supplied conditions]. [Independent comparison or sensitivity test] shows [second result]. The evidence [supports/indicates] [bounded interpretation or utility], although performance remains limited by [assumption, regime, coverage, or latency].`

## Key Points

- `[Main quantity] changes with [condition] over [verified regime]`
- `[Independent evidence] supports [bounded process] as a contributor to [phenomenon]`
- `[Method or dataset] improves [specific capability] under [validation conditions]`

Remove abbreviations and special characters, keep one complete claim per point, and verify the current 140-character limit before submission.

## Plain Language Summary

Build a new account for readers outside the subfield; do not translate the Abstract sentence by sentence.

`[Familiar description of the topic] matters because [scientific or societal relevance]. We examined [plain description of the question and approach]. We found [main result in accessible language], which [indicates/shows] [bounded meaning]. This finding matters for [supported broader relevance], although [plain-language limitation] remains.`

Use one paragraph of no more than 200 words for submission-ready text. Remove acronyms, equations, coordinate notation, channel labels, and specialist shorthand. Define the minimum unavoidable field-specific terms and preserve the difference between direct findings and interpretation.
